The Eiffel Tower is an iconic iron structure in Paris, France, built for the 1889 World's Fair.
It stands approximately 330 metres tall and was designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel.
The tower attracts around 7 million visitors annually.
It was built in 1885, two years before construction actually began.
The tower was originally made of steel, not wrought iron.
Due to its popularity as a tourist destination, it was never considered for demolition.
